5. Churn Rate by Customer Tenure

Customer retention often correlates with the time a customer has been with the service. Analyzed churn across three tenure buckets: New (0-6 mo.), Mid-Term (6-24 mo.), and Long-Term (24+ mo.).

  • Observation: New Customers (0-6 mo.) have the highest churn rate (35.20%), while Mid-Term and Long-Term customers show similar, lower rates (approx. 25%).
  • Actionable Insight: The initial 6-month period is the most critical for retention. Onboarding and initial engagement strategies must be prioritized to mitigate this early churn risk.
In[12]:
query_tenure_bucket = """
SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN tenuremonths < 6 THEN 'New (0-6 mo.)'
    WHEN tenuremonths < 24 THEN 'Mid-Term (6-24 mo.)'
    ELSE 'Long-Term (24+ mo.)'
  END AS tenure_group,
  ROUND(100.0 * AVG(churn), 2) AS churn_rate_percent
FROM customer_churn
GROUP BY tenure_group
ORDER BY churn_rate_percent DESC;
"""

6. Feature Correlation with Churn

To identify potential drivers, we examined the correlation between key quantitative features (Tenure Months, Total Spend, Support Tickets) and the likelihood of a customer churning.

  • Key Finding: The number of Support Tickets shows the strongest positive correlation with churn, indicating that high engagement with customer service is a significant churn predictor.
  • Context: Conversely, Tenure Months and Total Spend show a weak negative correlation, suggesting that as these values increase, churn slightly decreases. This reinforces the need to fix service issues quickly.
In[49]:
query_corr = """
SELECT
  ROUND(CORR(churn, tenuremonths)::numeric, 2) AS tenure_churn_corr,
  ROUND(CORR(churn, totalspend)::numeric, 2) AS spend_churn_corr,
  ROUND(CORR(churn, supporttickets)::numeric, 2) AS ticket_churn_corr
